My sourdough starter smells sharp and has a bit of liquid on top. Did I kill it?

Not at all — that’s a hungry starter, not a dead one. The liquid on top is hooch, which forms when the yeast has eaten through its food and the culture turns more acidic. The sharp smell is the same story.

Pour off the hooch (or stir it in for a tangier loaf), then get back on a regular feeding rhythm and it should bounce back within a day or two.

My sourdough starter smells sharp and has a bit of liquid on top. Did I kill it?

Not at all — that’s a hungry starter, not a dead one. The liquid on top is hooch, which forms when the yeast has eaten through its food and the culture turns more acidic. The sharp smell is the same story.

Pour off the hooch (or stir it in for a tangier loaf), then get back on a regular feeding rhythm and it should bounce back within a day or two.

How often should I feed it if I’m baking on the weekend?

If it lives on the counter, once a day is plenty — same time each morning works well. Keep it at roughly equal parts starter, flour, and water by weight.

The day before you bake, give it a second feeding in the evening so it’s at its peak and full of bubbles when you mix your dough. If you’d rather not feed daily, the fridge slows it right down to about once a week.
