My drums and bass sound muddy together. How do I make them sit better in the mix?

Muddiness usually lives in the low-mids around 200–400 Hz, where the kick and bass overlap. Pick one to own the low end — carve a gentle dip in the bass where the kick hits, and let the kick punch through.

A touch of sidechain compression on the bass, keyed to the kick, will open up space on every beat. Then high-pass everything that doesn’t need sub energy.
