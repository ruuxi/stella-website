Can you explain the difference between mitosis and meiosis? I keep mixing them up before my bio quiz.

The simplest way to keep them straight: mitosis makes two identical body cells for growth and repair, while meiosis makes four genetically unique sex cells with half the chromosomes.

So mitosis is about copying, and meiosis is about shuffling — crossing over and independent assortment mix the genes so no two gametes are alike. Want me to turn this into a few flashcards to drill before the quiz?

Mitosis vs. Meiosis
Mitosis = copying (2 identical diploid cells). Meiosis = shuffling (4 unique haploid gametes).
  • Crossing over → prophase I
  • Homologs separate in anaphase I
  • Independent assortment adds variation
  • No two gametes are genetically alike
