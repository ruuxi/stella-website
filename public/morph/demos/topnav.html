What's the best time to see tide pools at Point Reyes?

Aim for a negative low tide, which uncovers the most life and the deepest pools. These tend to cluster around new and full moons, and the lowest daytime lows in spring usually fall in the early morning.

Check a local tide table for the day you're planning, look for readings below −0.5 ft, and arrive about an hour before the low so you can work your way out as the water keeps dropping.
